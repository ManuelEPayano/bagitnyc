Proposal for #BagitNYC data collection program

  • Since 2013, we have been busy mapping plastic bag litter accross the city using Instagram hashtag #bagitnyc
  • To open the campaign to new participants and continue to impress the importance of this bill on City Council members, we will coordinate a data collection and advocacy program
  • Interns/volunteers will photograph and map plastic bag litter over the course of the program
  • Individual maps will be emailed to City Council members along with letters of support for the bill
  • Coalition member organizations are responsible for posting intern/volunteer positions and accrediting participants for their work
  • We will coordinate this as a mapping, policy and civic participation initiative

Timeline

  1. Collect feedback from coalition members
  2. Meet with coalition organization reps to finalize program and set up Instagram accounts
  3. Meet participants selected by organizations + CartoDB mapping workshop
  4. March 1st: Program Launch
  5. Weekly updates on participants' collection efforts via program portal website (coming soon)
  6. Additional/optional second workshop for participants keen to develop GIS and web development experience
  7. Final deliverable emailed to council reps by all participants, see next slide
  8. April 1st: Capstone Program Event - Plastic Bag Hunt in Bronx Districts.
    Program participants will help to coordinate the biggest effin' bag hunt yet, at this point equipped to chaperone younger mappers that may participate.

Sample Deliverable

                                                      Darren Klein
                                                      Brooklyn, NY
January 14, 2015
Dear City Council Member Robert Cornegy,

As constituents of your district, we are writing to you to express
our support for legislation to reduce plastic bag pollution, item 0209.  Over the past four weeks, 
we have participated in a program coordinated by BagItNYC that seeks to collect geographic and photographic data on plastic bag 
litter around the City. Below is a map of all plastic bags documented in Bedstuy, City Council District 36.  

In total, we photographed 270 plastic bags in your district. We believe your support for a 10 cent charge on all carryout bags can reduce the impact of plastic pollution in our community and make Bedstuy a reference for environmental guardianship. 

Thank you very much,
Darren Klein
Josselin Philippe
